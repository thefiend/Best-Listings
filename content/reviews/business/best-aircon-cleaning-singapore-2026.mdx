## What to Look for When Hiring an Aircon Cleaning Service in Singapore

**Google reviews and recency.** A 4.5+ rating with at least 50 reviews is the minimum threshold. More important is whether reviews are recent: an aircon company with 500 reviews but nothing posted in the past two months may have changed ownership or reduced service quality. Look for businesses with reviews from the current month before booking.

**Service scope clarity.** General servicing, chemical wash, and chemical overhaul are three distinct services with different price points and outcomes. A company that lists all three but cannot clearly explain the difference when asked is a warning sign. Reputable companies can tell you which service your unit actually needs based on symptoms, without defaulting to the most expensive option.

**Pricing transparency.** Trustworthy aircon companies in Singapore publish their prices online or provide an itemised quote before work begins. Quotes that only arrive after the technician is already on-site are a consistent pattern that leads to higher-than-expected bills. Compare at least two companies' quoted rates before booking any service.

**Licensing and registration.** Aircon companies carrying out gas-related work must comply with the Building and Construction Authority (BCA) registration requirements. Technicians handling refrigerants must hold the relevant certification. Ask whether the company and its technicians hold the appropriate licences, especially for chemical overhaul work that involves full unit disassembly.

**Response time.** Send a test enquiry via the company's website or WhatsApp and measure the response. For routine servicing, a reply within 24 hours is acceptable. For repairs, any company taking more than 4 hours to respond during business hours should be considered lower priority. How quickly a company responds to enquiries is a reliable indicator of how they communicate once a job is underway.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does aircon cleaning cost in Singapore?
General servicing costs SGD 25–70 per unit depending on the company and your location. Chemical wash runs SGD 80–150 per unit. A full chemical overhaul, which involves complete disassembly and deep cleaning, costs SGD 200–350 per unit. Most homeowners with three to four units spend SGD 100–200 for a full general service visit.

### How often should I clean my aircon in Singapore?
Every three months is the standard recommendation for residential units in Singapore, given the tropical humidity and year-round usage. Units in dusty environments, near construction sites, or in homes with pets may need cleaning every six to eight weeks. Commercial units running continuously should be serviced monthly.

### What is the difference between general servicing and chemical wash?
General servicing involves cleaning the air filter, fan coil, and drip tray without removing the unit from the wall. Chemical wash involves partial disassembly and flushing the coils, drain pan, and fan with chemical solution. General servicing is sufficient for well-maintained units on a quarterly schedule. Chemical wash is needed when a unit smells musty, cools poorly despite recent servicing, or has not been cleaned in over six months.

### What is a chemical overhaul and when do I need it?
A chemical overhaul is a full disassembly of the aircon unit: the fan coil, blower wheel, drain pan, and other components are removed and cleaned individually with chemical solution before reassembly. It is needed for units neglected for more than a year, units with mould growth inside, or units that continue to perform poorly after multiple chemical washes. Cost ranges from SGD 200 to SGD 350 per unit.

### How do I choose a good aircon cleaning company in Singapore?
Choose a company with at least 100 Google reviews at 4.5 stars or above, clearly published pricing or prompt itemised quotes, and a registered business address. Test their responsiveness by sending an enquiry before booking. Avoid companies that recommend the most expensive service without diagnosing the unit first, or that cannot explain what they are doing when asked.

### What questions should I ask before booking an aircon service?
Ask: What service does my unit need based on my description of symptoms? What is the total cost including GST and any additional charges? Are your technicians licensed for gas-related work? What is included and excluded in the quoted price? What is your policy if the unit performs poorly within two weeks of servicing?

### Is aircon servicing regulated in Singapore?
Yes. Aircon companies carrying out gas work must comply with the Singapore Refrigeration and Air Conditioning Contractors Association (RACCA) guidelines, and technicians handling refrigerants must be certified. The Building and Construction Authority (BCA) registers contractors for aircon and mechanical ventilation works. For major installations, ensure the company holds the appropriate BCA licence class.

### What are red flags when hiring an aircon cleaning company?
No fixed business address. Pricing that only arrives after the technician is on-site. Pressure to upgrade from general servicing to chemical overhaul without a clear diagnosis. No receipt or invoice after payment. Technicians who cannot explain the service they are performing. Quotes significantly below market rate, general servicing below SGD 20 per unit with no explanation.

### Can I get same-day aircon servicing in Singapore?
Yes, SoCool, 338 Aircon, and GoodCool all offer same-day bookings for general servicing when slots are available. Same-day availability is highest on weekday mornings and lowest on Saturdays. For emergency repairs, DW Aircon and Tech-V both advertise same-day emergency response for existing customers.

### How long does aircon cleaning take?
General servicing takes 20–30 minutes per unit. Chemical wash takes 45–60 minutes per unit. A full chemical overhaul takes 90–120 minutes per unit. For a three-bedroom HDB with three units, budget two to three hours for a full general service visit, or four to five hours for chemical wash across all units.
