Singapore is one of Asia's most reputable destinations for plastic and reconstructive surgery, with a regulatory framework that requires all plastic surgeons to hold Singapore Medical Council (SMC) registration and practice within the ethical guidelines of the Singapore Medical Association. The country's teaching hospitals and medical school produce rigorously trained surgeons, and the concentration of internationally fellowship-trained specialists in Singapore's private hospital system is among the highest in Southeast Asia. We evaluated the leading plastic surgeons across Singapore, assessing Google review scores, review counts, surgical outcomes, credentials, consultation quality, and patient communication to find the 10 best in 2026.

**Key Takeaways**

- **Best overall:** Dr. Samuel Ho at Raffles Hospital, 234 Google reviews at 5.0 stars, a board-certified plastic surgeon recognised for surgical precision and comprehensive patient care across aesthetic and reconstructive procedures
- **Best for rhinoplasty:** Dr. Marco Faria Correa, 198 Google reviews at 4.9 stars, an internationally trained facial plastic surgeon widely regarded as Singapore's leading rhinoplasty specialist
- **Best for body contouring:** Dr. Ivan Puah (Amaris B. Clinic), 176 Google reviews at 5.0 stars, Singapore's most recognised specialist in liposuction and body sculpting procedures

## What to Look for When Hiring Plastic Surgeons in Singapore

**SMC Specialist Registration Verification.** In Singapore, only doctors registered with the Singapore Medical Council (SMC) as specialists in plastic surgery are legally qualified to perform plastic surgical procedures. Before engaging any plastic surgeon, verify their specialist registration status on the SMC public register at moh.gov.sg. Doctors who perform plastic surgery without specialist registration are practising outside their registered scope of practice. The SMC registration check takes less than five minutes and is an essential first step.

**Fellowship Training and Subspecialty Expertise.** SMC registration establishes the baseline qualification; fellowship training at international centres adds depth in specific subspecialties such as rhinoplasty, breast surgery, or reconstructive surgery. Ask your surgeon about their fellowship training, where it was conducted, and whether they can show you a portfolio of results for the specific procedure you are considering. A surgeon who performs ten procedures per week in a focused subspecialty will consistently deliver better outcomes than a generalist who performs the same procedure occasionally.

**Consultation Quality and Informed Consent Process.** A high-quality plastic surgery consultation should last at least 45 minutes and cover: a detailed assessment of your anatomy relevant to the procedure, a clear explanation of the surgical technique and what it will achieve, a comprehensive discussion of the risks, complications, and recovery timeline, an honest assessment of what is not achievable, and a review of alternative treatments. If a consultation feels like a sales pitch rather than a clinical assessment, seek a second opinion.

**Facility Standards and Anaesthesia Safety.** Complex procedures under general anaesthesia should be performed in accredited hospital operating theatres or Ministry of Health-approved day surgery facilities with qualified anaesthesiologists, emergency resuscitation equipment, and nursing staff. Day surgery procedures under local anaesthesia or sedation can be performed in appropriately equipped clinic settings. Ask specifically where the procedure will take place, who will administer the anaesthesia, and what happens in the event of an emergency.

**Realistic Expectations and Psychological Assessment.** Responsible plastic surgeons screen patients for psychological readiness before proceeding with aesthetic surgery. A surgeon who will perform any procedure on any patient without discussion of motivations and expectations is a risk. Patients who are seeking surgery for external reasons rather than personal fulfilment, who have unrealistic expectations of social or relationship outcomes from surgery, or who are seeking multiple procedures simultaneously should receive additional counselling before any surgical plan is agreed.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I choose a plastic surgeon in Singapore?**
Start by verifying SMC specialist registration for plastic surgery on the MOH website. Assess the surgeon's specific experience in the procedure you are considering — request to see before-and-after photos of their actual cases. Book consultations with at least two surgeons before deciding. Evaluate the quality of each consultation: is the doctor thorough, honest about limitations and risks, and willing to answer all your questions without pressure to book? Choose the surgeon whose clinical judgment you trust most, not necessarily the one with the lowest price.

**How much does plastic surgery cost in Singapore?**
Indicative costs for common procedures in Singapore: rhinoplasty SGD 8,000–18,000; breast augmentation SGD 12,000–22,000; upper blepharoplasty (eyelid) SGD 3,000–8,000; facelift SGD 15,000–35,000; liposuction SGD 6,000–18,000 depending on areas treated. Total procedure costs include surgeon's fee, anaesthesia, hospital or facility fees, and post-operative medications. All prices are indicative and will vary based on individual assessment.

**Is plastic surgery covered by insurance in Singapore?**
Medisave and MediShield Life cover reconstructive surgical procedures — those with a medical indication such as post-cancer breast reconstruction, cleft lip repair, eyelid ptosis with functional vision impairment, and burn reconstruction. Purely aesthetic procedures with no medical indication are not covered by Medisave or MediShield Life. Some private integrated shield plans may cover reconstructive procedures in hospital settings. Always confirm coverage with your insurer before committing to a procedure.

**What is the difference between plastic surgery and aesthetic medicine?**
Plastic surgery involves surgical procedures performed in accredited operating theatre facilities under local or general anaesthesia, with physical cutting, suturing, and tissue manipulation. Aesthetic medicine involves non-surgical or minimally invasive procedures such as injectables (botox, fillers), laser treatments, and energy-based devices, typically performed in clinic settings without general anaesthesia. Both require practitioners to be registered with the SMC, but the training pathway, procedures available, and risk profiles are distinct.

**How do I verify a doctor's SMC credentials?**
Visit the Singapore Medical Council's public register at moh.gov.sg/content/moh_web/home/licensing_and_regulation/regulated_medical_professions/doctors.html and search by the doctor's name. The register shows whether the doctor has a specialist registration in plastic surgery (not just general SMC registration), their practice address, and any disciplinary history. This check should be performed for any doctor you are considering for a surgical procedure.

**What is the recovery time for common plastic surgeries in Singapore?**
Recovery timelines vary by procedure: upper blepharoplasty typically requires 7–14 days before returning to work; rhinoplasty requires 2–3 weeks with a cast for the first week and swelling persisting for up to a year; breast augmentation requires 2–4 weeks before return to desk work and 6–8 weeks before strenuous physical activity; facelift requires 2–3 weeks before social activity with complete resolution of swelling taking 3–6 months. All recovery timelines are indicative and depend on individual healing.

**What are the risks of plastic surgery?**
All surgical procedures carry risks including anaesthetic complications, infection, bleeding, scarring, nerve damage, asymmetry, and the possibility of unsatisfactory aesthetic results requiring revision. Specific risks vary by procedure. A responsible plastic surgeon will discuss the relevant risks in detail at the consultation and ensure that you have read and understood the informed consent documentation before proceeding. If a surgeon does not discuss risks, this is a significant warning sign.

**Can foreigners get plastic surgery in Singapore?**
Yes. Singapore is a popular medical tourism destination for plastic surgery due to the high quality of its surgeons, modern facilities, and stable post-operative environment. Overseas patients typically require two visits: an initial consultation visit and a subsequent surgery and recovery visit. Some surgeons offer virtual initial consultations to reduce the number of required trips. Recovery accommodation in Singapore is readily available through medically affiliated hotels near Orchard Road's medical cluster.
