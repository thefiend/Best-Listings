## What to Look for When Hiring a Maid Agency in Singapore

**MOM licensing status.** All employment agencies in Singapore that place domestic workers must hold a valid EA Licence from MOM. Verify your agency's licence on MOM's Employment Agencies Directory before paying any fees. Agencies with licence conditions, suspensions, or revocation history should be avoided regardless of their marketing claims. A licensed agency is also subject to MOM's Employment Agencies Act regulations on fee caps and conduct standards.

**Helper bio-data and vetting process.** Any credible agency should provide complete bio-data for shortlisted helpers including education, previous work experience (with employer contact details if available), medical history declarations, training certifications, and a recent photograph. Agencies that cannot or will not provide complete bio-data before placement are not conducting adequate pre-screening.

**Replacement policy terms.** The standard industry replacement period is three to six months from placement date. Review the specific conditions — some agencies require the employer to demonstrate MOM compliance as a condition of replacement, which is reasonable. Be wary of agencies whose replacement clauses contain multiple conditions that effectively make replacement difficult to invoke in practice.

**Post-placement support.** The first 30–90 days of a new placement are the period where most compatibility issues emerge. An agency that provides proactive follow-up contact — not just a reactive complaints line — can identify and address adjustment issues before they escalate to placement failure. Ask specifically what post-placement support the agency provides and how they handle situations where the employer or helper has a concern.

**Fee transparency.** MOM's EA Act caps the fees agencies can charge both employers and helpers. Ask for a complete fee breakdown before signing any agreement — placement fee, work permit application fee, medical examination cost, insurance premium, and any administrative charges. Agencies that quote a single all-inclusive number without itemisation are not giving you the transparency you need to compare fairly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What are the MOM requirements for hiring a domestic helper in Singapore?

The employer must be a Singapore Citizen, Permanent Resident, or Employment Pass holder aged 21 or above. A security bond of SGD 5,000 is required for non-Malaysian foreign domestic workers (typically covered by insurance). The employer must purchase personal accident insurance (minimum SGD 60,000 medical coverage) and provide free medical examination annually. Mandatory rest days (one per week minimum, or salary compensation for rest days worked with helper's written consent) apply. Employers must complete the Employer's Orientation Programme (EOP) before the helper's work permit is approved.

### How much does it cost to hire a maid in Singapore?

Typical costs for a new overseas placement: agency fee SGD 1,200–2,500, work permit application SGD 35 (MOM fee), medical examination SGD 80–120, personal accident insurance SGD 250–350 per year, security bond insurance SGD 150–200 per year. Ongoing monthly costs: helper salary (typically SGD 600–800 for Filipino/Indonesian helpers, SGD 500–650 for Myanmar), food allowance, accommodation costs, and annual medical checkup. Total annual cost including salary ranges from SGD 9,000 to SGD 14,000 depending on helper nationality and household.

### What is the difference between a fresh helper and a transfer helper?

A fresh helper is arriving from their home country on their first Singapore work permit. They typically have no Singapore work experience but may have household experience in their home country. A transfer helper is already in Singapore, currently employed or recently between employers, and transfers to a new employer within Singapore. Transfer helpers bring Singapore work experience and verifiable local employment history but may be between employers for various reasons that should be investigated before placement.

### Can I hire a helper from any country?

Singapore allows domestic helpers from a list of approved source countries including Philippines, Indonesia, Myanmar, Sri Lanka, India, Bangladesh, Thailand, and several others. Each source country has different typical salary ranges, skills profiles, and language abilities. Filipino helpers typically have stronger English language skills; Indonesian helpers are common in Malay-speaking households; Myanmar helpers have been increasingly placed in recent years. Discuss source country preferences and their practical implications with your agency.

### What should I do if my helper and I have a dispute?

Contact MOM's Tripartite Alliance for Dispute Management (TADM) for mediation assistance. Do not terminate the work permit without following MOM's procedures — premature cancellation without cause can result in employer obligations for the helper's return airfare and salary-in-lieu of notice. If the issue is serious misconduct, document all incidents and consult your agency before taking any employment action. The Centre for Domestic Employees (CDE) also provides dispute mediation services.

### What happens when my helper's work permit expires?

Work permits for foreign domestic workers are issued for two years and can be renewed. Notify MOM and begin the renewal process at least two months before expiry. Renewal requires a current medical examination, valid personal accident insurance, and confirmation of continued employment eligibility. The work permit renewal is processed by MOM online and does not require agency involvement for existing helpers with clean employment records.
