Singapore's property market is one of the most regulated and transaction-intensive in Asia, with the Council for Estate Agencies (CEA) overseeing all property agents and requiring mandatory registration before any individual can represent buyers, sellers, landlords, or tenants. We evaluated Singapore's leading property agencies by assessing Google ratings, review volume, agent network size, transaction track record, and the breadth of market segments covered. This list covers agencies equipped to handle HDB resale, private residential, luxury property, commercial real estate, new launch, and expatriate relocation transactions in Singapore.

**Key Takeaways**

- **Best overall:** ERA Realty Network Pte Ltd, 1,876 Google reviews at 4.9 stars, Singapore's largest property agency by agent count with the most comprehensive island-wide coverage
- **Most reviewed:** PropNex Realty Pte Ltd with 2,341 Google reviews at 4.8 stars, the highest review volume of any property agency in Singapore
- **Best for luxury properties:** Knight Frank Singapore, 234 Google reviews at 4.8 stars, the premier agency for ultra-high-end residential transactions above SGD 5 million
- All property agents in Singapore must be registered with CEA — always verify an agent's registration on the CEA Public Register at cea.gov.sg before engaging them for any transaction
- Standard commission rates are approximately 1% from the seller and 1% from the buyer for private property; HDB resale co-broking is typically 1% per side. New launch buyers do not pay commission directly (it is paid by the developer)
- Red flag: agents who cannot produce their CEA registration number on request, or who pressure you to sign an Exclusive Estate Agency Agreement before providing meaningful advice

## What to Look for When Hiring Property Agents in Singapore

**CEA registration verification.** Every property agent in Singapore must be registered with the Council for Estate Agencies (CEA). Verify your agent's registration on the CEA Public Register at cea.gov.sg before signing any documents or providing any payment. The register shows the agent's name, registration number, agency affiliation, and whether any disciplinary action has been taken. This verification step takes under two minutes and is essential.

**Individual agent track record, not just agency brand.** The quality of your property transaction depends almost entirely on your individual agent, not the agency they work for. Ask your prospective agent for their personal transaction history — how many properties have they transacted in your target area in the past 12 months, what were the sale prices versus asking prices, and how long did units take to sell. An agent with strong local area knowledge and a proven recent transaction history is far more valuable than a famous brand represented by an inexperienced agent.

**ABSD and eligibility planning.** Singapore's Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty (ABSD) significantly affects the cost of buying second and subsequent properties for Singapore citizens, PRs, and foreigners. Ensure your agent provides accurate ABSD planning advice before you commit to a purchase, particularly if you already own one or more properties. ABSD mistakes cannot be reversed after the option to purchase is exercised.

**Commission transparency.** Standard commission rates in Singapore are approximately 1% of transaction price per side for private property. HDB co-broking is approximately 1% per side. New launches do not charge buyers commission (paid by developer). Ask your agent explicitly about commission at the outset of the engagement and request written confirmation of the agreed fee structure before viewing any properties.

**Exclusive versus non-exclusive agreements.** Agents may ask you to sign an Exclusive Estate Agency Agreement that gives them the sole right to market your property for a defined period. Exclusive agreements can be beneficial — the agent has greater incentive to invest in marketing — but review the terms carefully, including the duration, the marketing obligations, and the circumstances under which you can terminate the agreement. Never sign an exclusive agreement before the agent has demonstrated knowledge of your property and the local market.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I choose a property agent in Singapore?

Start by verifying the agent's CEA registration on the public register. Then assess their personal transaction history in your target area and property segment over the past 12 months. Request an initial consultation to evaluate their market knowledge, communication style, and whether their pricing advice is realistic. Obtain references from recent clients if possible before signing any agency agreement.

### What commission do property agents charge in Singapore?

For private property sales, standard commission is approximately 1% of transaction price from the seller and 1% from the buyer. For HDB resale, co-broking commission is approximately 1% per side. New launch buyers pay no commission — it is built into the developer's marketing costs. Commission rates are negotiable in some cases, particularly for higher-value transactions or where one agent represents both parties.

### What is CEA registration and why does it matter?

The Council for Estate Agencies (CEA) is Singapore's regulatory body for property agents. All property agents must be registered with CEA and hold a valid registration to legally act for buyers, sellers, landlords, or tenants in property transactions. An unregistered person who acts as a property agent commits a criminal offence. Always check the CEA Public Register before engaging any property agent in Singapore.

### What is the difference between HDB and private property agents in Singapore?

Most property agents in Singapore handle both HDB and private property, but the transactions involve different regulatory frameworks. HDB transactions require knowledge of eligibility rules, CPF housing grants, HDB loan eligibility, resale levy calculations, and minimum occupation period requirements. Private property transactions involve different stamp duty considerations, fewer eligibility restrictions, and different financing options. An agent with recent HDB transaction experience should have clear familiarity with current HDB policies.

### What is ABSD and how does it affect buying property in Singapore?

Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty (ABSD) is a tax levied on the purchase price of residential properties by certain buyers. Singapore citizens purchasing their second residential property pay 20% ABSD; their third and subsequent properties attract 30% ABSD. Singapore PRs pay 5% ABSD on their first purchase, 30% on their second. Foreigners pay 60% ABSD on any residential property purchase. ABSD does not apply to HDB flats purchased directly from HDB, commercial, or industrial properties.

### How long does a property transaction take in Singapore?

For HDB resale transactions, the typical timeline from Option to Purchase to completion is two to three months, including the mandatory HDB appointment and funding arrangement. Private residential transactions typically complete in eight to twelve weeks from option grant to legal completion. New launch purchases involve a booking fee payment on launch day followed by the exercise of the Sale and Purchase Agreement within five weeks, with legal completion typically one to three years later when the development is completed.

### What questions should I ask a property agent before engaging them?

Ask for their CEA registration number and verify it independently. Ask how many properties they have transacted in your target area in the past 12 months, and for evidence of those transactions. Ask their opinion on the correct asking or offer price and why. Ask about the current buyer or tenant demand for your property type. Ask how they will market your property and what their commission is. Ask how quickly they typically respond to client enquiries.

### Can I buy property in Singapore without an agent?

Yes. You can purchase a resale HDB flat or private property without engaging an agent. However, the absence of an agent means you are responsible for all aspects of the transaction — price negotiation, option drafting review, legal process coordination, and dealing with the seller's agent without your own representation. Most buyers benefit from having their own agent, particularly for HDB transactions where eligibility rules and CPF procedures are complex. An agent representing only the seller has a duty to their client, not to you.
