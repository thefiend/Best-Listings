## What to Look for When Hiring a Pest Control Company in Singapore

**NEA licensing verification.** All pest control companies operating in Singapore must hold a valid Pest Control Operator (PCO) licence issued by NEA. Individual technicians must also hold NEA Pest Control Operator certification. Verify your company's licence at NEA's directory before engaging. Companies with licence conditions, suspensions, or enforcement actions should be avoided regardless of price or marketing.

**Pest identification before treatment.** A professional pest controller should identify the specific pest species before recommending a treatment — German cockroach vs American cockroach, for example, require different treatment approaches, as do Formosan vs Asian subterranean termites. A technician who applies a one-size treatment without first conducting an inspection and pest identification is not providing professional service.

**Chemical safety disclosure.** Ask for the Safety Data Sheets (SDS) for any chemicals to be applied in your property. Any licensed PCO is required to be able to provide these. Check the re-entry interval after treatment (typically four hours for most indoor treatments), food preparation surface safety requirements, and pet safety precautions. If a technician cannot or will not disclose the chemicals being used, do not allow the treatment to proceed.

**Treatment warranty.** Professional pest control companies should offer a warranty period following treatment — typically 30–90 days for cockroach and general pest treatment, 12 months for termite treatment. Understand the warranty conditions (what triggers a re-treatment, what evidence is required, what exclusions apply) before signing any service agreement.

**Annual contract vs one-off treatment.** For common Singapore household pests (cockroaches, ants, mosquitoes), an annual contract with quarterly visits and unlimited emergency response is typically more cost-effective than repeated one-off treatments for recurring infestations. A one-off treatment addresses the current infestation but does not prevent re-infestation from neighbouring units or outdoor populations. Annual contracts provide ongoing preventive treatment that suppresses re-infestation before it becomes visible.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What are the most common pests in Singapore?

German cockroaches (the most common indoor pest), Aedes mosquitoes (dengue vector), subterranean termites (structural damage risk), roof rats and Norway rats, common ants (particularly crazy ants and ghost ants), bed bugs (increasingly common in high-turnover residential and hospitality environments), and house lizards (geckos — harmless but unwelcome in some households). Seasonal factors: dengue mosquito risk peaks during wet season (November to January and May to July).

### How often should I do pest control in Singapore?

For residential HDB flats: quarterly general pest treatment is standard for households with no active infestation, monthly for households in buildings with known infestation clusters. For private property with gardens: monthly mosquito larviciding and quarterly general treatment. For food and beverage businesses: monthly at minimum, with many regulatory environments requiring more frequent documented visits. For termite management: annual inspection plus ongoing monitoring if baiting stations are installed.

### Is fogging effective against mosquitoes?

Thermal fogging kills adult mosquitoes present at the time of treatment but provides only temporary population suppression. It does not affect mosquito larvae in breeding sites or prevent re-infestation. Effective mosquito management requires larviciding (treating water breeding sites) to prevent new adults from hatching, combined with habitat modification (eliminating standing water). Fogging alone treats the symptom; larviciding and habitat modification address the cause.

### How do I know if I have termites?

Common signs of termite infestation in Singapore: mud tubes on walls or skirting boards (subterranean termites build these as travel paths), hollow-sounding timber when tapped, discarded wings near window and door frames (from swarmers, typically in April–June), small holes in timber, and powdery frass (termite excrement that resembles fine sawdust). If you see any of these signs, call a termite specialist immediately — colonies can cause significant structural damage within six to twelve months of establishment.

### Can I do pest control myself?

For minor ant trails or isolated cockroach sightings, over-the-counter gel baits (for cockroaches) and ant bait stations can be effective. However, for established infestations, recurring problems, or termites, professional treatment is required — over-the-counter products typically achieve temporary knockdown but do not address the colony or harbourage points that cause re-infestation. NEA regulations also restrict certain pesticides to licensed PCO application only.

### What should I do to prepare for a pest control treatment?

Remove food items from treatment areas, cover pet water bowls and food, remove pets from treatment rooms, ensure children are out of the treatment area for the re-entry period (typically four hours), clear under-sink areas for cockroach gel bait application, and remove items from floor areas the technician needs to access. For termite treatments, some furniture movement may be required — confirm with the technician during the pre-treatment survey.
