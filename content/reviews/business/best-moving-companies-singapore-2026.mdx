## What to Look for When Hiring a Moving Company in Singapore

**Written quotation with itemised costs.** Never accept a verbal quote for a move. Insist on a written quotation that specifies: number of crew, number of vehicles, packing materials included, furniture disassembly and reassembly, any applicable surcharges (weekend, public holiday, high-floor, long-distance), and deposit terms. The gap between verbal and written quotes is where disputes originate. Any mover who declines to provide a written quotation should be immediately disqualified.

**Insurance coverage.** Professional movers carry goods-in-transit insurance that covers items damaged during the move. Confirm the coverage amount, the claims process, and whether the coverage is automatic or an optional add-on. For high-value items — antiques, artworks, jewellery, electronics — ask specifically whether your item category is fully covered under the standard policy or requires a declared value rider. Do not assume all-risk coverage if you have not confirmed it in writing.

**Crew experience and consistency.** The crew that appears on moving day is the primary quality variable in a move. Ask whether the company uses employed crews or subcontracted labour. Movers who rely heavily on subcontractors have less control over crew quality and training standards. The best movers on this list use consistent, trained employed crews who understand their company's service standards.

**Loading and delivery timing.** In Singapore's condo market, elevator booking windows and loading bay time restrictions are real constraints that affect move timing. Confirm that your mover has experience in your building or building type, will coordinate with the management office, and arrives within the confirmed time window. A mover who is two hours late on move day causes a cascade of elevator booking, parking, and handover timing problems that cannot be easily recovered.

**Post-move damage process.** Before the crew leaves, inspect your items and sign the delivery note only after confirming all items have arrived and visible damage is documented. Any damage discovered must be reported to the mover's damage claim process, typically within 24–48 hours of delivery. Understand the process before move day — ask the mover specifically: if something is damaged, what is the claims process and what is the compensation basis?

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a local move cost in Singapore?

A 2-room HDB move typically costs SGD 250–400, a 3-room HDB SGD 350–600, a 4-room HDB SGD 500–800, and a 5-room or executive flat SGD 700–1,200. Private property 3-bedroom condo moves typically cost SGD 700–1,400 depending on floor level and volume. Full-pack services (where the mover packs all items) add SGD 150–400 to these estimates. Weekend and public holiday surcharges of 20–30% apply with most movers.

### How far in advance should I book a moving company in Singapore?

For weekday moves, two weeks advance booking is typically sufficient. For weekend or public holiday moves, or moves at month-end when HDB lease transitions peak, four to six weeks advance booking is recommended. For international shipments, allow six to eight weeks for sea freight or two to three weeks for air freight, including the time for pre-move survey, packing, and customs documentation.

### What should I pack myself vs leave for the movers?

Pack yourself: irreplaceable documents (passport, birth certificates, property deeds), jewellery and small high-value items, medications, and items you want readily accessible on move day. Leave for the movers: all furniture, large appliances, boxed household goods, clothing, and kitchenware — professional packers work faster and more safely than most homeowners. Do not pack hazardous materials (paint, gas canisters, batteries, flammable liquids) — movers are legally required to decline these regardless of how they are packed.

### What is the difference between sea freight and air freight for international moves?

Sea freight (FCL or LCL) is the standard method for household goods shipments — transit time ranges from two to six weeks depending on destination, and cost is significantly lower than air freight for volume shipments. Full Container Load (FCL) provides exclusive use of a container and is suitable for a family's complete household goods. Less than Container Load (LCL) shares container space and is suitable for smaller shipments. Air freight is faster (three to seven days) but costs four to six times more per cubic metre and is typically used only for urgent items or small shipments.

### Do I need to be present during the move?

For packing, ideally yes — you can direct the crew on priorities, identify fragile items, and ensure items you want to transport yourself are set aside. For the move itself, at minimum ensure a responsible adult is present at both origin and destination. For international shipments, you will need to sign the inventory list at the origin packing and the delivery receipt at destination.
