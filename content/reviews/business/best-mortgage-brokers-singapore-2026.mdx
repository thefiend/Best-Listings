## What to Look for When Hiring a Mortgage Broker in Singapore

**Bank panel breadth.** A broker's value is directly tied to how many bank relationships they maintain. A broker with access to only five or six banks cannot credibly claim to have found the market's best rate. The strongest brokers on this list access 20 or more bank packages, covering both local banks (DBS, OCBC, UOB) and the full range of foreign banks active in Singapore's mortgage market.

**Transparent fee disclosure.** All reputable Singapore mortgage brokers are paid by the bank, not the borrower. If a broker asks for an upfront advisory fee or a percentage of the loan, that is a significant red flag. Confirm at the first meeting that the service is free to you and that the broker's compensation does not influence their bank recommendation.

**TDSR competence.** The Total Debt Servicing Ratio is the MAS regulation that caps your monthly loan repayments across all liabilities at 55% of gross income. A broker who cannot walk you through a TDSR calculation, or who does not proactively check your TDSR position before recommending a quantum, is not providing complete advice. This is basic knowledge for any credible mortgage professional.

**Proactive rate monitoring.** The best brokers in Singapore do not disappear after the loan is drawn down. Look for brokers who offer rate monitoring and will alert you when your lock-in period is ending and refinancing would produce material savings. This ongoing service is the difference between a transactional broker and a long-term financial advisor.

**Client reviews with specific outcomes.** Generic five-star reviews are easy to accumulate. The most reliable signal is reviews that mention specific dollar amounts saved, specific problems solved, or specific scenarios handled. If a broker's reviews read like testimonials rather than actual experiences, the review base may not reflect genuine client outcomes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do mortgage brokers get paid in Singapore?

Mortgage brokers in Singapore are paid a commission by the bank whose package is taken up, not by the borrower. This commission is built into the bank's cost of customer acquisition and does not increase the interest rate charged to the borrower. The service is therefore free to use from the borrower's perspective. Always confirm this arrangement at the start of any broker engagement.

### What documents do I need for a home loan in Singapore?

For salaried employees: latest three months' payslips, last two years' income tax Notice of Assessment (NOA), CPF contribution history (last twelve months), NRIC, and the property option or purchase agreement. For self-employed: last two years' NOA, business registration documents, bank statements, and sometimes company financial accounts. Foreigners may need passport, employment pass, and overseas tax returns.

### HDB loan vs bank loan — which is better?

HDB concessionary loan offers a stable rate (pegged at CPF OA rate plus 0.1%, currently 2.6%), up to 80% LTV, and full flexibility to switch to a bank loan later. Bank loans typically offer lower initial rates (SORA-pegged, currently 2.5–3.5%) but have lock-in periods, variable rates, and require a minimum 5% cash downpayment. First-timer buyers are eligible for HDB loans on BTO and resale flats subject to income ceiling criteria.

### What is SORA and how does it affect my mortgage?

SORA (Singapore Overnight Rate Average) is the benchmark rate that replaced SIBOR and SOR for Singapore dollar floating-rate loans from 2024. Bank mortgage rates are expressed as SORA plus a fixed spread (e.g., 1M SORA + 0.80%). When SORA rises or falls, the borrower's effective rate changes accordingly at each rate reset period (monthly or quarterly, depending on the loan). Fixed-rate packages lock the rate for two to three years regardless of SORA movement.

### What is TDSR and how is it calculated?

Total Debt Servicing Ratio (TDSR) is the MAS regulation limiting total monthly debt repayment obligations to 55% of the borrower's gross monthly income. All loans are counted: home loan, car loan, credit card minimum payments, renovation loans, personal loans. If your TDSR exceeds 55%, the loan quantum will be reduced or the application declined. A broker should calculate your TDSR position before recommending a loan quantum.

### Can a foreigner get a home loan in Singapore?

Yes, foreigners can obtain bank loans for private residential properties in Singapore (HDB purchases are restricted to Singapore Citizens and PRs). LTV ratios for foreigners are typically the same as for citizens (75% for first property) subject to TDSR compliance. Foreign income documentation requirements are more extensive — most banks require employment pass validity of at least one year beyond the loan application date and overseas income verification.

### What is the best time to refinance my mortgage?

The optimal time to refinance is shortly before your loan's lock-in period expires, typically in the final one to two months of the lock-in. Refinancing during the lock-in period incurs a penalty (typically 1.5% of outstanding loan amount). Monitor market rates six months before your lock-in expires and engage a broker three months out to allow time for comparison, application, and legal processing. Savings of SGD 15,000–40,000 over the new package tenure are achievable with a 0.3–0.5% rate improvement on a SGD 700,000 loan.

### How long does mortgage approval take in Singapore?

In-principle approval (IPA) from most Singapore banks takes three to seven working days with complete documentation. Full loan approval after exercising the option to purchase (OTP) typically takes one to three weeks. Conveyancing and drawdown complete the process over six to eight weeks for private property, and three to four months for BTO completion. Self-employed applicants or those with complex income structures may take longer.
