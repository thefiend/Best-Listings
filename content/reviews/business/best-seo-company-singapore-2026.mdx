## What to Look for When Hiring an SEO Company in Singapore

**Google reviews and recency.** A 4.5-plus rating with 50 or more reviews is a reliable baseline for a Singapore SEO agency. More important than the average is recency. An agency with 300 five-star reviews but nothing posted in the last three months may have reduced its service quality or changed account teams. Look for consistent reviews posted within the past 60 to 90 days.

**Transparency on methodology.** Legitimate SEO agencies explain exactly what they do: which technical fixes they prioritise, how they build links, what content they produce, and how they measure progress. If an agency cannot clearly explain its process on a sales call, that is a red flag. Vague promises of "proprietary algorithms" or "exclusive ranking techniques" have no basis in how Google actually ranks content.

**Case studies with verifiable results.** Ask for specific examples from clients in your industry with comparable site authority and competition levels. The best agencies can show before-and-after screenshots of Search Console data, keyword rank history, and traffic attribution. An agency that declines to share any case study evidence has something to hide.

**Contract terms and exit clauses.** Singapore SEO retainers typically run on 6 to 12-month contracts. Understand what happens to your content, link profile, and Google Business Profile access if you leave. Agencies that retain ownership of content they produce on your behalf, or who link build through profiles they control, create exit risk worth negotiating before signing.

**Response time and reporting cadence.** Test the agency before signing. Send an enquiry and measure the response time. A fast, prepared response to a prospect signals how they treat paying clients. Monthly reporting should include keyword rank changes, organic traffic delta, conversion attribution, and a written commentary on what changed and why, not just a raw data dump.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does SEO cost in Singapore?

Full-service SEO retainers in Singapore typically range from SGD 600 to SGD 5,000 per month depending on scope, competition, and agency size. Budget agencies serving SMEs start around SGD 500 to 800 per month. Mid-market agencies with dedicated account managers and content production typically charge SGD 1,200 to 2,500 per month. Enterprise-level campaigns with aggressive link acquisition can exceed SGD 5,000 monthly.

### How long does SEO take to show results in Singapore?

Expect 3 to 6 months before meaningful ranking improvements appear for most competitive keywords. Technical fixes and Google Business Profile optimisation can produce visible results within 4 to 8 weeks. Long-tail keywords with lower competition often rank within 2 to 3 months. Highly competitive terms in finance, legal, or healthcare typically require 6 to 12 months of consistent effort.

### What questions should I ask an SEO agency before signing a contract?

Ask: What specific keywords will you target in the first 90 days? How do you build links and can I see examples? Who writes the content, in-house or outsourced? What does the monthly reporting include? What happens to the content and links if I cancel? Have you worked with businesses in my industry before?

### Do I need a local Singapore SEO agency or can I use an overseas one?

For businesses targeting Singapore search results, a local agency has meaningful advantages. They understand Google Singapore's local ranking signals, Google Business Profile optimisation for the Singapore Maps pack, and Singapore-specific content context. Overseas agencies often lack the local backlink relationships and cultural understanding needed for competitive Singapore keywords.

### What is the difference between technical SEO and content SEO?

Technical SEO addresses how search engines crawl, index, and render your site. Core areas include site speed, mobile optimisation, structured data, canonicalisation, and crawl budget. Content SEO addresses what is on your pages. Both are required. A technically perfect site with thin content will not rank. Well-written content on a slow, poorly structured site will underperform its potential.

### What are red flags when hiring an SEO company in Singapore?

No physical Singapore office address. Guarantees of first-page rankings within 30 days. Pricing that is 70 percent below market rate with no explanation. Reluctance to share past client results or references. Contracts that assign them ownership of content or link profiles they build. Monthly reports that show only traffic numbers with no keyword or conversion detail.

### Can SEO guarantee first-page rankings on Google?

No. No agency can guarantee specific keyword rankings because Google's algorithm is determined by over 200 signals outside any agency's control, including competitor activity, algorithm updates, and user engagement patterns. Reputable agencies guarantee process, effort, and transparent reporting. Any agency that guarantees a specific ranking should be treated with scepticism.

### How do I measure ROI from SEO services?

Track: organic sessions to your site via Google Analytics, keyword rank changes for target terms via Search Console or a rank tracker, and conversions attributed to organic search via Analytics goals or CRM attribution. A simple formula: if organic search generates 20 leads per month at a 20 percent conversion rate and SGD 500 average deal value, organic revenue is SGD 2,000 per month. Compare this against your monthly SEO retainer to calculate ROI.

### What is the typical contract length for SEO services in Singapore?

Most reputable Singapore agencies require 6 to 12-month minimum contracts because SEO produces compounding results over time, not immediate returns. Month-to-month contracts are available at some boutique agencies but often carry a premium. Before signing a 12-month contract, confirm what is deliverable in months 1 to 3 versus months 4 to 12 so you have clear milestones to evaluate progress.

### How often should my SEO agency report to me?

Monthly reporting is standard. The report should include keyword rank changes for all tracked terms, organic session delta versus the prior period, top landing pages by organic traffic, and a written commentary on what changed and why. Quarterly strategic reviews where campaign direction is assessed are best practice for retainers over SGD 1,500 per month.
