## What to Look for When Hiring a Home Renovation Contractor in Singapore

**Google reviews and recency.** A 4.5-star Google rating with 50 or more reviews is the minimum credibility threshold for renovation contractors in Singapore. More important than the aggregate average is recency: a firm with 300 five-star reviews but nothing posted in the past 6 months may have changed ownership or declined in quality. Prioritise contractors with a consistent stream of recent reviews from clients who mention specific project types similar to yours.

**Direct contractor vs. interior design firm.** Interior design firms charge a design fee on top of renovation costs, typically 10 to 20% of total project value, in exchange for design services, 3D renders, and project management. Direct contractors skip the design layer and focus on execution, which is better value for homeowners who already have a clear design direction. The right choice depends entirely on how much design input you need.

**Pricing transparency and itemised quotes.** Reputable renovation contractors provide detailed, line-item quotations covering hacking, carpentry, tiling, painting, electrical, plumbing, and sanitary fixtures individually. Lump-sum quotes without itemisation make cost comparisons impossible and create disputes during project variations. If a contractor resists providing an itemised quote, that is a clear disqualifying signal.

**Project management inclusion.** Ask whether the contractor assigns a dedicated project coordinator or site supervisor throughout the project. In Singapore's renovation industry, quality issues most commonly arise not from trade skill but from coordination failures between multiple sub-trades. A contractor who manages their own workforce and holds regular site reviews reduces this risk significantly.

**HDB licensing.** All renovation contractors working on HDB flats must be registered with HDB as a Renovation Contractor and must obtain an HDB renovation permit before works commence. Verify that any contractor you engage has current HDB RC registration, which can be checked on the HDB portal. Non-compliance can result in fines and mandatory reinstatement of unauthorised works at the homeowner's expense.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does home renovation cost in Singapore?

A standard 4-room HDB renovation in Singapore typically costs SGD 30,000 to SGD 60,000 depending on scope, materials, and whether you engage a direct contractor or an interior design firm. 5-room HDB renovations range from SGD 40,000 to SGD 80,000. Condo renovations generally start at SGD 50,000 and can exceed SGD 150,000 for full-scope landed projects. Direct contractors typically price 15 to 20% below interior design firms for comparable scope.

### What is the difference between a renovation contractor and an interior design firm in Singapore?

A renovation contractor executes physical works including hacking, carpentry, tiling, painting, and M&E. An interior design firm provides design services (space planning, material selection, 3D renders) and project management in addition to overseeing construction. Interior design firms charge a design fee and mark up contractor costs; direct contractors bill for works only. If you have a clear design vision, a direct contractor delivers better value. If you need design input and coordination, an interior design firm earns its premium.

### How long does a home renovation take in Singapore?

A full 4-room HDB renovation typically takes 6 to 8 weeks from hacking to completion. 5-room HDB and condo renovations take 8 to 12 weeks. Landed property renovations range from 3 to 6 months depending on scope. Factors that extend timelines include custom carpentry lead times, tile orders from overseas suppliers, and HDB permit processing, which typically takes 3 to 5 working days.

### Do I need HDB approval for renovation works?

Yes. All structural, hacking, electrical, and plumbing works in HDB flats require a renovation permit from HDB before works commence. Your contractor is responsible for applying for the permit on your behalf. Non-structural cosmetic changes such as painting and minor fixture replacement typically do not require a permit. Any contractor who starts hacking or structural work without an HDB permit is operating illegally and exposes you to reinstatement liability.

### What questions should I ask a renovation contractor before signing?

Ask: Are you registered as an HDB Renovation Contractor? Can you provide an itemised quotation? Who is my dedicated project manager or site supervisor? What is your payment schedule and how do variation orders work? What warranty do you provide on workmanship and materials? How many projects will you run concurrently during mine? Can I visit a recently completed site?

### How far in advance should I book a renovation contractor in Singapore?

For popular contractors with strong review track records, book 3 to 6 months in advance. The best firms fill their project slots 4 months out during peak periods, particularly in the first half of the year when BTO flat handovers are concentrated. Booking with less than 6 weeks' notice typically limits your choice to smaller or newer contractors with limited track records.

### Are renovation contractors in Singapore licensed?

Contractors working on HDB flats must hold an active HDB Renovation Contractor registration, renewed annually and verifiable on the HDB website. Contractors performing electrical works must employ licensed electrical workers. Gas works must be performed by Singapore-licensed gas service workers. There is no general licensing requirement for contractors operating exclusively on private residential properties, which is why review counts and verified track records are important for condo and landed homeowners.

### What are red flags when hiring a renovation contractor in Singapore?

Red flags include: requests for more than 20% upfront before works commence, resistance to providing itemised quotations, no verifiable physical business address, suspiciously similar Google reviews clustered in a short window, no HDB RC registration for an HDB project, inability to provide references from recent clients, and verbal agreements rather than a written contract covering scope, timeline, payment schedule, and variation order process.

### Can I renovate my HDB flat while living in it?

Minor works such as painting and fixture replacement are compatible with occupied living. Hacking, carpentry fabrication, and M&E works generate significant dust, noise, and disruption. HDB restricts noisy works to Monday to Friday 9am to 5pm and Saturday 9am to 1pm. Most homeowners vacate or stay with family during the main renovation phase.

### What is a typical payment schedule for renovation work in Singapore?

A standard schedule is: 20% upon contract signing and permit application, 40% upon commencement of main works, 30% upon substantial completion, and 10% upon final handover and defect rectification. Any contractor requesting more than 50% before works are substantially underway is a significant risk. Always ensure the payment schedule and variation order process are documented in a written contract before any payment is made.
