## What to Look for When Hiring a Roof Contractor in Singapore

**BCA registration and MCST approval.** Roof contractors in Singapore should hold a valid BCA contractor registration for their scope of work. For structural works, canopy additions, or A&A (Addition and Alteration) projects, a Professional Engineer (PE) endorsement is legally required. MCST-approved status is essential if you are managing an HDB estate or strata-titled property. Always verify BCA registration before signing a contract.

**Google review count and recency.** A 4.5+ rating with at least 20 reviews is the minimum baseline for a legitimate contractor. More important than the average rating is recency. A contractor with 150 reviews but nothing posted in the last 3 months may have changed hands or reduced operations. Prioritise contractors with a consistent stream of recent reviews from the past 3 months.

**Itemised written quotes.** Reputable contractors provide written quotations that separate labour, materials, and disposal costs. Vague lump-sum quotes are a yellow flag. The difference between a targeted repair and a full roof replacement in Singapore can be SGD 5,000 to 20,000, so quote clarity is essential before any work begins. Get at least two itemised written quotes before committing to any contractor.

**Workmanship warranty.** Established contractors offer a 12-month to 5-year workmanship warranty on new installations, separate from material warranties. Ask specifically: what is covered, what voids the warranty, and how is a claim initiated? Get this in writing before signing any contract. A contractor who cannot provide a written workmanship warranty for a full roof replacement is not worth the risk.

**Response time to initial enquiry.** During Singapore's northeast monsoon, demand for roofing contractors spikes significantly. A contractor who takes more than 48 hours to respond to an initial enquiry will take the same or longer to respond during a live leak. Test response speed before committing. Fast initial contact strongly correlates with reliable emergency availability.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does roof repair cost in Singapore?
Minor roof leak repairs typically cost SGD 200–500. Waterproofing membrane application for a flat roof runs SGD 1,500–4,000 depending on area. Full roof replacements for a standard terrace house cost SGD 8,000–20,000 depending on material and roof size. Always get at least two itemised written quotes before committing to any contract.

### How do I find a reliable roof contractor in Singapore?
Search Google Maps for "roof contractors Singapore" and filter by 4.5+ stars with a minimum of 20 reviews. Check their BCA registration status, ask for references from similar property types, and send a test enquiry to measure response time. Contractors who provide itemised written quotes, respond within 24 hours, and hold BCA registration are consistently the strongest choices.

### What should I ask before hiring a roof contractor?
Ask: Are you BCA-registered and what is your registration grade? Can you provide an itemised written quote? What workmanship warranty do you offer? Do you handle permit applications for A&A works? Are you MCST-approved? What is the payment schedule, and when is final payment due? How soon can you start?

### How often should a roof be inspected in Singapore?
At minimum once a year, ideally before the northeast monsoon (November–January) when sustained heavy rainfall most commonly exposes weak points. Properties over 15 years old benefit from twice-yearly inspections. For HDB flats, roof maintenance of common areas is managed by the Town Council, but owners of landed property are personally responsible for their entire roof structure.

### What are common causes of roof leaks in Singapore?
The most common causes are: failed waterproofing membrane on flat roofs, cracked or displaced roof tiles on pitched roofs, blocked drains and gutters causing standing water, deteriorated parapet wall flashing, and cracks at the junction between roof surface and structural walls. In Singapore's climate, thermal expansion and contraction from daily temperature swings accelerate deterioration across all of these areas.

### Do roof contractors in Singapore need to be licensed?
Standard maintenance and repair work requires a valid BizSafe certification and BCA contractor registration. For structural works, additions, or alterations requiring planning permission, a Qualified Person (QP) and often a Professional Engineer (PE) endorsement are required. Verify the contractor's BCA registration status directly on the BCA website before signing any contract for significant structural work.

### What is the difference between roof repair and roof waterproofing?
Roof repair addresses structural failures: cracked tiles, damaged flashing, broken gutters, or sagging sections. Roof waterproofing applies a protective membrane or coating to prevent water ingress regardless of whether visible structural damage is present. Most leaks in Singapore require both: structural repair to fix the cause, then waterproofing to seal the surface. Applying waterproofing over unrepaired structural damage will result in the problem recurring within one to two monsoon seasons.

### Can I get same-day emergency roof repair in Singapore?
Yes. Contractors including The Roofing Man, Roof Guru, and SWC Construction offer emergency or same-day response for urgent situations. Response depends on current workload and weather conditions, but most established contractors can deploy a team within 24 hours for confirmed leaks. During the peak November–January monsoon period, expect 24–48 hours even for priority calls due to high seasonal demand.

### How long does a roof replacement take in Singapore?
A standard terrace house roof replacement of 150–200 sqm typically takes 3–7 working days from materials delivery to completion. Flat roof membrane replacement for a condominium penthouse unit may take 2–4 days. Timeline depends on material type, site accessibility, current weather, and contractor availability. Rain delays are common and contractually unavoidable, so factor this into scheduling around key dates.

### What are red flags when hiring a roof contractor?
No BCA registration or BizSafe certification. No physical Singapore address listed on their website or Google Maps profile. Lump-sum quotes with no breakdown of materials and labour. Demand for full payment before work starts. Refusal to provide a written workmanship warranty. Reviews that repeat the same generic phrases without specific project detail.
