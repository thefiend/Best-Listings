Singapore's solar market has matured significantly over the past five years, driven by the government's SolarNova programme, falling panel costs, and growing awareness among homeowners and businesses of solar's long-term economics. With an average irradiance of approximately 1,580 kWh per square metre per year and a government that has set a target of at least 2 GWp of solar deployment by 2030, Singapore is one of the most compelling solar markets in Southeast Asia. We evaluated the leading solar installation companies across Singapore, assessing Google review scores, review counts, panel quality, installation standards, after-sales service, and financial transparency to find the 10 best providers in 2026.

**Key Takeaways**

- **Best overall:** Sunseap Leasing Pte Ltd, 234 Google reviews at 4.9 stars, Singapore's largest solar operator with over 1 GWp of deployed capacity across residential, commercial, and industrial segments
- **Best for commercial and industrial:** Sembcorp Solar Singapore, 187 Google reviews at 4.8 stars, the energy infrastructure giant with unmatched financial stability and project delivery capability
- **Best for HDB and residential:** Solargy Pte Ltd, 112 Google reviews at 5.0 stars, Singapore's highest-rated residential solar specialist
- A typical 5 kWp residential system costs SGD 8,000–15,000 installed, with a payback period of 7–12 years depending on household consumption patterns
- Singapore's Enhanced Central Intermediary Scheme (ECIS) allows households and businesses to sell excess solar generation — always confirm your installer can handle the SP Group application
- Red flag: installers who cannot provide a projected energy yield calculation based on your actual roof area and orientation, or who are unable to show examples of completed EMA-compliant installations

For residential clients, their rooftop solar packages are available as outright purchase or lease, with a dedicated customer service team handling the end-to-end process from site assessment through to grid connection. Sunseap's combination of scale, regulatory expertise, and service infrastructure makes them the top choice for any client prioritising reliability and accountability.

## What to Look for When Hiring Solar Companies in Singapore

**EMA Licensing and SP Group Compliance.** All solar installers in Singapore must be registered with the Energy Market Authority (EMA) to legally connect systems to the national grid. Verify that your installer holds a valid EMA licence before engaging them. Additionally, if you want to benefit from Singapore's Enhanced Central Intermediary Scheme (ECIS) to sell excess generation back to the grid, confirm that your installer can handle the SP Group application process, which involves technical submissions and metering upgrades.

**Projected Energy Yield and Financial Modelling.** A credible solar installer will provide a written energy yield projection based on your specific roof orientation, tilt angle, available area, and local irradiance data. Be wary of installers who provide estimates based on generic assumptions without a site-specific assessment. Ask for the projected annual generation in kWh, the assumed panel degradation rate, and a payback period calculation based on your actual electricity tariff.

**Panel Quality and Warranty Terms.** Solar panels are a 25-year investment and the quality of the product matters significantly over that horizon. Evaluate the panel manufacturer's reputation, the efficiency rating of the panels being proposed, and the warranty terms — specifically whether the power output warranty is a linear guarantee (preferred) or a step-function guarantee. Panels from manufacturers with established Singapore presences, such as REC and Maxeon, offer stronger warranty enforcement prospects than panels from less established brands.

**Post-Installation Monitoring and Maintenance.** A solar system that is not monitored may underperform for months without the owner realising it. Ask whether the installer provides a monitoring platform that tracks daily generation, flags underperformance against projected yield, and alerts the installer to faults. Confirm what the service response time is for system faults and whether maintenance visits are included in any ongoing service agreement.

**Total Cost of Ownership and Incentive Awareness.** Singapore does not currently offer feed-in tariffs, but the ECIS allows you to sell excess generation at the prevailing market rate. Understanding the economics of self-consumption versus export is important when sizing a system. Ask your installer to model both scenarios for your property. Additionally, confirm whether your commercial project qualifies for any BCA Green Mark or Enterprise Development Grant (EDG) incentives that could improve the project economics.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is solar worth it in Singapore?**
Yes, for most homeowners and businesses with suitable rooftop space. Singapore's high irradiance of approximately 1,580 kWh per square metre per year, combined with residential electricity tariffs around SGD 0.30 per kWh, typically produces payback periods of 7–12 years for a properly sized system. With panel lifespans of 25–30 years, the return on investment is positive for the majority of Singapore properties. The economics are strongest for landed residential properties and commercial buildings with large, well-oriented rooftops.

**How much does solar installation cost in Singapore?**
A typical 5 kWp residential system costs SGD 8,000–15,000 installed, depending on panel brand, system configuration, and installation complexity. Commercial systems are priced per kWp at decreasing rates as system size increases, typically SGD 1,200–2,000 per kWp for systems above 50 kWp. Obtain multiple quotes and compare on a cost-per-kWp basis to ensure you are receiving a competitive price for the panel quality being proposed.

**Can HDB flats install solar panels?**
HDB residents can participate in solar through the SolarNova programme, which deploys solar panels on HDB block rooftops and allows residents to benefit from the energy generated without installing individual panels. For HDB owners who want individual household systems, this is only possible for HDB maisonettes with private rooftop access. Standard HDB flat owners do not have access to individual rooftop space but can benefit from common area solar through their town council's participation in SolarNova.

**What is the payback period for solar in Singapore?**
The typical payback period for a residential system in Singapore is 7–12 years, depending on system size, household consumption, roof orientation, and whether the household maximises self-consumption of solar generation. Commercial systems with larger scale benefits and higher consumption can achieve payback periods of 5–8 years. Battery storage adds to the upfront cost but can shorten the payback period by increasing self-consumption and reducing peak demand charges.

**How does net metering work in Singapore?**
Singapore operates the Enhanced Central Intermediary Scheme (ECIS), under which households and businesses with solar can sell excess generation to the grid at the prevailing half-hourly market rate, which is generally lower than the retail tariff. There is no premium feed-in tariff in Singapore; excess export is sold at wholesale market rates. The financial benefit of solar in Singapore therefore comes primarily from self-consumption rather than export, making system sizing relative to consumption an important consideration.

**What solar panel brands are best for Singapore's climate?**
The best panel brands for Singapore's tropical climate are those with demonstrated performance in high-temperature, high-humidity conditions and strong warranty enforcement capability. REC, Maxeon, Jinko Solar, LONGi, and Canadian Solar are all well-regarded brands with active presences in Singapore. Maxeon and REC panels are generally considered premium options for their efficiency and warranty terms. Avoid panels from manufacturers with no regional presence, as warranty claims in Singapore will be difficult to enforce.

**Do I need EMA or SPS approval for solar in Singapore?**
Yes. All grid-connected solar systems in Singapore require EMA approval through the SP Group connection process. Your installer must submit technical documentation to SP Group, and a licensed electrical worker must commission and sign off the completed installation. The process typically takes 4–8 weeks from application to approval. Standalone off-grid systems (not connected to the national grid) do not require SP Group approval but must still comply with BCA and fire safety requirements.

**What maintenance does a solar system require?**
Solar systems require minimal ongoing maintenance. Panels should be cleaned every 3–6 months to remove dust and bird droppings that can reduce generation by up to 10%. Inverters — the component most likely to require replacement — typically have 10–12 year lifespans and should be monitored for error codes via the system's monitoring platform. Annual system inspections by a qualified electrician are recommended to check mounting integrity, cable condition, and inverter performance. Most installers offer annual maintenance packages for SGD 150–400.
