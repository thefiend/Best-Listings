Commercial cleaning in Singapore operates within a structured regulatory framework: all cleaning businesses must be licensed by the National Environment Agency (NEA), and the industry has faced heightened standards since COVID-19 drove demand for enhanced disinfection and environmental hygiene protocols. Whether you are managing a corporate office, an industrial facility, a healthcare environment, or a space that has just undergone renovation, the quality and reliability of your cleaning contractor directly affects the health and productivity of everyone who uses the space. We evaluated the leading commercial cleaning companies across Singapore, assessing Google review scores, review counts, NEA compliance, service breadth, and client retention to find the 10 best providers in 2026.

**Key Takeaways**

- **Best overall:** ISS Facility Services Singapore, 234 Google reviews at 4.8 stars, Singapore's most established integrated facility services provider with operations across every commercial segment
- **Best for post-renovation cleaning:** Jansen Environmental Services, 156 Google reviews at 5.0 stars, the top-rated specialist for construction site and post-renovation deep cleaning
- **Best eco-friendly cleaning:** GreenTech Cleaning Solutions, 78 Google reviews at 5.0 stars, the highest-rated sustainable cleaning specialist using certified green chemicals

- Red flag: cleaning contractors who cannot produce a valid NEA business licence or who quote prices verbally without providing a written scope of work and contract

**NEA Business Licence Verification.** All commercial cleaning businesses in Singapore must hold a valid licence from the National Environment Agency (NEA). Before signing a cleaning contract, ask for the company's NEA licence number and verify its status on the NEA licensing portal. Unlicensed cleaning operators are not permitted to legally provide cleaning services in Singapore, and using an unlicensed contractor exposes your business to regulatory risk.

**Scope of Work Clarity.** A well-structured cleaning contract specifies exactly what will be cleaned, how frequently, with what products, and to what standard. Generic cleaning contracts that describe only broad task categories without specifying frequencies, areas, and expected outcomes are difficult to enforce and lead to disputes about service delivery. Require a detailed scope of work document before signing, and ensure that periodic deep cleaning tasks (kitchen degreasing, bathroom descaling, floor stripping) are listed with their own frequencies.

**Staff Training and Turnover Management.** The quality of commercial cleaning depends on the skill and consistency of the cleaners assigned to your premises. Ask about the company's training programme, particularly for specialist tasks, and enquire about their staff turnover rate — high turnover means you will frequently be training new staff in your building's specific requirements. Companies that assign dedicated teams to each client's premises, rather than rotating staff, deliver more consistent results.

**Chemical Safety and Eco-Compliance.** The cleaning chemicals used in your premises affect the health of your staff, visitors, and the cleaners themselves. Ask for the Safety Data Sheets (SDS) for the products your contractor uses, and verify that any disinfectants or specialist chemicals are approved for use in your specific environment. For healthcare, food service, or childcare facilities, chemical selection is a compliance requirement, not just a preference.

**Contract Flexibility and Performance Management.** Commercial cleaning contracts vary widely in their terms regarding scope changes, performance remedies, and termination. Look for contracts that include clear performance standards, a formal complaint process with stated response times, and the ability to adjust scope as your business needs change without punitive variation charges. Avoid contracts that lock you into fixed scope for more than 12 months without a performance review clause.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does commercial cleaning cost in Singapore?**
General office contract cleaning in Singapore typically costs SGD 800–3,000 per month depending on floor area, cleaning frequency, number of cleaners deployed, and the scope of services included. One-time deep cleaning ranges from SGD 150–500 for residential premises and SGD 500–5,000 for commercial spaces. Always compare quotes on a like-for-like basis — ensure each quote covers the same scope, frequency, and cleaning products before comparing prices.

**What is the difference between contract cleaning and one-time cleaning?**
Contract cleaning involves a regular scheduled service under an ongoing agreement, typically covering daily or multi-weekly cleaning of your premises. One-time cleaning is a single engagement for deep cleaning, post-renovation cleaning, or a specific cleaning project without an ongoing commitment. Contract cleaning is generally more cost-effective per visit than one-time cleaning, and the assigned team develops familiarity with your space over time.

**Do cleaning companies need to be NEA licensed in Singapore?**
Yes. All cleaning businesses operating in Singapore must hold a valid NEA business licence under the Environmental Public Health (EPH) Act. The licence must be renewed annually, and companies can be found on the NEA's public registry. Using an unlicensed cleaning contractor is a regulatory risk for both the contractor and the client engaging them.

**What is deep cleaning vs regular cleaning?**
Regular cleaning covers daily maintenance tasks: vacuuming, mopping, surface wiping, washroom servicing, and waste removal. Deep cleaning is a periodic, more intensive service covering areas not addressed in regular cleaning: inside appliances, behind furniture, grout lines, high-level surfaces, air conditioning filters, and upholstery. Deep cleaning is recommended quarterly for most commercial spaces and semi-annually for residential premises.

**How do I set up a cleaning contract for my office?**
Begin by assessing your cleaning requirements: floor area, number of people, washroom facilities, kitchen facilities, frequency needed, and any specialist surfaces. Obtain at least three quotes from NEA-licensed contractors with a detailed scope of work for each. Review the contract terms for performance standards, termination notice, and scope change procedures. Start with a trial period of one to three months before committing to a longer contract term.

**What chemicals are used in commercial cleaning?**
Commercial cleaning uses a range of chemical categories: surface detergents for general cleaning, disinfectants (quaternary ammonium compounds or hydrogen peroxide-based) for sanitisation, degreasers for kitchen surfaces, descalers for washrooms, glass cleaners, and floor care products including sealers and polishes. Eco-certified alternatives exist for all these categories and are increasingly used by green-focused cleaning companies. Request Safety Data Sheets for any chemicals used on your premises.

**How often should an office be cleaned?**
Most Singapore offices require daily cleaning covering floor mopping, washroom servicing, surface wiping, and waste removal. Pantry and kitchen areas may require twice-daily attention in larger offices. Carpets should be deep cleaned every three to six months. Windows should be cleaned monthly for interior surfaces and quarterly for exterior surfaces. High-touch surfaces such as door handles and elevator buttons should be disinfected daily as a minimum.

**What is post-renovation cleaning and when is it needed?**
Post-renovation cleaning is a specialist deep clean conducted after construction, renovation, or fitting-out work to remove construction dust, debris, chemical residues, adhesive remnants, and protective coatings before the space is occupied. It should be conducted after all building work is fully complete, before furniture and equipment are moved in. A professional post-renovation clean typically takes half a day to two days for a residential property and several days for a large commercial space, depending on the extent of the renovation.
