## What to Look for When Hiring Aesthetic Clinics in Singapore

**MOH Licensing and Doctor Credentials.** In Singapore, aesthetic treatments involving lasers, injectables (botox, fillers), and energy-based devices must be performed by or under the direct supervision of an MOH-licensed medical doctor. Before undergoing any aesthetic treatment, ask specifically who will be performing the procedure — the doctor or a therapist — and verify that the doctor holds valid SMC registration and has specific training in the treatment being offered. The MOH has issued public advisories against receiving injectable treatments from unlicensed practitioners.

**Consultation Quality.** A responsible aesthetic clinic will conduct a thorough medical consultation before recommending any treatment. This should include an assessment of your skin or area of concern, a review of your medical history and current medications, an explanation of the treatment options available, an honest discussion of expected outcomes and limitations, and a clear explanation of the risks and recovery involved. Consultations that are rushed or focused primarily on promoting treatments rather than assessing your needs are a warning sign.

**Technology and Equipment Standards.** The efficacy of laser, HIFU, and radiofrequency treatments depends significantly on the quality and calibration of the equipment used. Ask specifically which device will be used for your treatment, what the device's brand and model is, and how long the clinic has had the equipment. Well-maintained equipment from recognised manufacturers (Solta Medical, Syneron-Candela, Fotona, Cynosure) will consistently outperform older or off-brand alternatives, even in the hands of a skilled operator.

**Post-Treatment Support and Follow-Up.** The best aesthetic clinics provide structured post-treatment care including written aftercare instructions, a follow-up assessment at an appropriate interval to evaluate results, and accessible contact channels for questions or concerns that arise after treatment. Clinics that do not conduct follow-up assessments or that are difficult to reach after you have paid are prioritising revenue over patient outcomes.

**Red Flags and Patient Safety.** Be wary of clinics that offer aesthetic treatments at prices significantly below the market average — these typically reflect the use of diluted products, counterfeit injectables, or lower-quality equipment. The MOH has prosecuted clinics in Singapore for using counterfeit botulinum toxin and unregistered filler products; always ask your clinic to confirm the product brand and registration status for any injectable treatment. If a clinic refuses to provide this information, do not proceed with treatment.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the difference between an aesthetic clinic and a plastic surgery clinic?**
An aesthetic clinic offers non-surgical and minimally invasive treatments such as injectables (botox, fillers), laser treatments, energy-based skin treatments (HIFU, radiofrequency), and medical-grade facials. A plastic surgery clinic offers surgical procedures performed under local or general anaesthesia with physical cutting and suturing. Some clinics offer both under the same roof with a doctor who holds both aesthetic medicine training and surgical specialist accreditation.

**How do I know if an aesthetic clinic is legitimate in Singapore?**
Check that the clinic is registered with MOH as a licensed medical clinic, that the treating doctor holds valid SMC registration, and that the products being used are HSA (Health Sciences Authority) registered. The MOH publishes a list of licensed medical clinics in Singapore. You can verify a doctor's SMC registration at moh.gov.sg. Be cautious of clinics that cannot provide their MOH licence number or the HSA registration numbers for injectable products.

**What treatments are only allowed by doctors in Singapore?**
In Singapore, the following treatments must be performed by or under the direct supervision of a licensed doctor: botulinum toxin injections, dermal filler injections, laser treatments (all classes), energy-based treatments including HIFU, radiofrequency, and high-intensity light, and chemical peels above a prescribed strength. Beauticians and therapists are not permitted to administer these treatments. Receiving injections from an unlicensed practitioner is a violation of Singapore's Healthcare Services Act.

**How much does Botox cost in Singapore?**
Botulinum toxin treatment in Singapore typically costs SGD 300–800 per area treated (forehead, frown lines, crows' feet), depending on the product used (Botox, Dysport, Xeomin, Nabota) and the clinic's pricing. Prices below SGD 200 per area may indicate diluted product or an unregistered product. All prices are indicative and subject to individual assessment — the amount of product required varies by patient anatomy.

**What is HIFU and is it effective?**
HIFU (High-Intensity Focused Ultrasound) is a non-surgical treatment that delivers focused ultrasound energy to targeted depths within the skin, stimulating collagen production and causing controlled thermal coagulation that results in tissue tightening over the following three to six months. Ultherapy is the most widely used HIFU platform in Singapore. HIFU is most effective for patients with mild to moderate skin laxity who want non-surgical tightening without the recovery of surgery. Results are real but modest compared to surgical lifting, and the treatment is most effective when repeated annually.

**Are aesthetic treatments painful?**
Pain levels vary significantly by treatment. Botulinum toxin injections are generally well tolerated with minimal discomfort using fine needles. Filler injections can cause brief sharp discomfort, mitigated by topical numbing cream and lidocaine contained in most modern filler products. Laser treatments range from mild warmth to significant heat sensation depending on the intensity setting. HIFU can be uncomfortable during the treatment, particularly over bony areas. Most clinics offer topical numbing cream for treatments on sensitive areas.

**How often should I get aesthetic treatments?**
Treatment frequency depends on the specific procedure and your skin's response. Botulinum toxin typically lasts 3–6 months before retreatment is needed. Hyaluronic acid fillers last 6–18 months depending on product and area treated. Laser skin rejuvenation is typically recommended in a series of 3–5 sessions spaced 3–4 weeks apart, followed by annual maintenance. HIFU is typically performed once or twice per year. Your doctor should design a treatment schedule based on your individual skin condition and treatment goals.

**What should I check before getting fillers in Singapore?**
Before receiving filler treatment in Singapore, confirm: that the clinic is MOH-licensed and the treating doctor is SMC-registered; the brand and HSA registration number of the filler product to be used; that the doctor has experience with the specific filler technique being offered; that a full medical history review and skin assessment has been conducted; that the risks including bruising, swelling, asymmetry, and rare vascular complications have been discussed; and that the clinic has hyaluronidase available to dissolve filler in the event of an adverse reaction.
