## What to Look for When Hiring Corporate Lawyers in Singapore

**Relevant practice area experience.** Corporate law in Singapore covers an enormous range of work — M&A, capital markets, banking, employment, IP, and regulatory compliance are all distinct disciplines. Ensure the firm you engage has a dedicated practice group with verifiable transaction experience in your specific area of need, not just a generalist corporate practice that handles everything superficially.

**Law Society verification.** Before engaging any lawyer in Singapore, verify their practising certificate and the firm's status on the Law Society of Singapore's public directory. All practising Singapore lawyers must hold a current annual practising certificate and be admitted to the Singapore Bar. This check takes two minutes and protects you from engaging unqualified practitioners.

**Partner accessibility and delegation practices.** Large firms may pitch work at partner level but deliver it at associate level. Ask specifically who will be handling your matter day-to-day, what the partner's involvement will be at each stage, and whether the quoted rate reflects the seniority of the person actually doing the work.

**Fee structure transparency.** Singapore corporate legal fees are typically billed by the hour, but fixed-fee arrangements are available for routine work such as company incorporations, standard shareholder agreements, and employment contract reviews. Request a written fee estimate at the outset, and ask about disbursements and administrative charges that are not included in the headline rate.

**Commercial versus purely technical advice.** The best corporate lawyers in Singapore do not simply tell you what the law permits — they advise you on what course of action best serves your commercial objectives within the law. Look for practitioners who ask questions about your business context before giving advice, and be cautious of lawyers who provide only technical analysis without helping you understand the practical implications.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I choose a corporate lawyer in Singapore?

Start by identifying your specific legal need — incorporation, M&A, fundraising, employment, or regulatory compliance — and look for firms with a dedicated practice in that area. Check the Law Society of Singapore's public register to verify the lawyer's standing, review their published deal track record, and request an initial consultation to assess whether their communication style and commercial approach suit your business.

### What services do corporate lawyers provide in Singapore?

Corporate lawyers in Singapore handle company incorporations and secretarial matters, mergers and acquisitions, joint ventures, capital markets transactions (IPOs, rights issues, bonds), employment law, shareholder agreements, commercial contracts, intellectual property assignments, regulatory licensing, and corporate governance advisory. Full-service firms also handle related litigation and arbitration when commercial disputes arise.

### How much do corporate lawyers cost in Singapore?

Associate billing rates at tier-one firms range from SGD 350 to SGD 900 per hour, while senior partners bill at SGD 800 to SGD 2,000+ per hour. Mid-tier and boutique firms typically charge SGD 300 to SGD 600 per hour for partners. Fixed-fee arrangements are available for routine matters such as company incorporations (from SGD 500) and standard shareholder agreements (from SGD 2,000).

### What is the difference between a solicitor and advocate in Singapore?

In Singapore, all practising lawyers are admitted as both advocates and solicitors. "Advocate" refers to the right to appear in court; "solicitor" refers to non-contentious legal work such as drafting contracts and providing advice. Unlike some Commonwealth jurisdictions, Singapore does not maintain a split legal profession — the same lawyer can both advise and litigate.

### When do I need a corporate lawyer in Singapore?

You should engage a corporate lawyer when incorporating a company with multiple shareholders, raising external investment, entering into significant commercial agreements, acquiring or selling a business, listing on a stock exchange, structuring employment arrangements for senior management, or responding to regulatory investigations or shareholder disputes.

### How do I verify a lawyer's credentials in Singapore?

Visit the Law Society of Singapore's website and use its public directory to confirm that a lawyer holds a current practising certificate. The directory also shows the firm the lawyer is practising at. You can also check the Singapore Legal Counsel directory on the Ministry of Law's iLAB system for additional registration information.

### What is a retainer agreement in Singapore?

A retainer agreement is a contract between a client and a law firm that sets out the scope of legal services to be provided, the billing rate or fixed fees, the payment terms, and the conditions under which the engagement can be terminated. In Singapore, all law firms are required to provide a written warrant to act or engagement letter before commencing substantive work on a matter. Review this document carefully before signing, particularly the billing and dispute resolution clauses.

### Can foreign companies use Singapore lawyers?

Yes. Foreign companies incorporated outside Singapore can engage Singapore law firms for advice on Singapore law matters and for transactions governed by Singapore law. Singapore law firms can also coordinate with foreign counsel for cross-border matters. However, Singapore lawyers are not authorised to advise on foreign law — foreign law advice must come from lawyers admitted in the relevant jurisdiction.
