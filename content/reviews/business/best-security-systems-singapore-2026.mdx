## What to Look for When Hiring Security Systems Companies in Singapore

**Police Licensing and Regulatory Compliance.** Singapore's Police Licensing and Regulatory Department (PLRD) requires all security agencies providing guarding services to be licensed. Before engaging any security provider, verify their status on the PLRD public registry. For electronic systems installers who do not provide guarding, check that their installations meet SS 553 and SS 554 standards, which are mandated for commercial properties by the Building and Construction Authority.

**Monitoring Infrastructure Ownership.** There is a significant difference between providers who own and operate their own central monitoring stations and those who outsource monitoring to third parties. Providers with in-house monitoring stations — such as SECOM and Ademco — offer greater accountability and faster response coordination. Always ask where your alarm signals will be monitored, who will respond, and what the contractual guaranteed response time is.

**System Scalability and Integration Capability.** A security system that cannot grow with your needs will require costly replacement within a few years. Assess whether the provider's platform supports IP cameras, analytics modules, access control, and smart building integration within a single management interface. Open-platform systems using ONVIF-compatible products give you the flexibility to mix components from different manufacturers without being locked into a single vendor's ecosystem.

**After-Sales Support and Warranty Terms.** The quality of post-installation support is as important as the installation itself. Verify the warranty terms on hardware components (typically two to five years), the availability of on-site support for faults, and the response time SLA for monitoring centre alerts. Providers with a local Singapore technical team will always outperform those relying on regional service hubs for on-site fault resolution.

**Total Cost of Ownership.** Compare the full cost over a three-to-five year horizon, not just the installation price. Include monitoring fees, maintenance contract costs, hardware warranty extensions, and software licensing. Some providers offer low installation prices but high monitoring fees that make the total cost significantly higher than a comparable offering with transparent all-inclusive pricing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What security system is best for a Singapore HDB flat?**
For HDB flats, a standalone IP CCTV system covering the main door and common corridor, combined with a smart video doorbell and door sensors, is the most practical solution. Full alarm monitoring systems are less common in HDB due to proximity to neighbours and noise concerns. Providers such as DSS Security and Total Security Management offer packages specifically designed for residential flats with minimal installation disruption.

**How much does CCTV installation cost in Singapore?**
Residential CCTV installation typically costs SGD 800–2,500 depending on the number of cameras, whether cabling is surface-mounted or concealed, and whether cloud or local recording is chosen. Commercial CCTV installations range from SGD 2,000 for a small shopfront to SGD 30,000 or more for a multi-floor office building with analytics capabilities. Always obtain at least three quotes and confirm that the price includes installation labour, cabling, a recorder, and basic configuration.

**Do I need a licence to install security cameras in Singapore?**
Homeowners and businesses do not need a licence to install security cameras on their own private property. However, cameras must not point into neighbouring properties, common areas not under your ownership, or public areas in a way that could infringe on others' privacy under Singapore's Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA). Security companies providing guarding services must hold a valid PLRD licence, but pure electronic systems installers are not required to be licensed.

**What is the difference between IP and analog CCTV?**
IP (Internet Protocol) cameras transmit digital video over a network, allowing higher resolution, remote viewing, integration with analytics software, and flexible storage options including cloud recording. Analog cameras transmit a continuous analogue signal to a digital video recorder (DVR) and are generally lower resolution and less flexible. Most new installations in Singapore now use IP cameras exclusively; analog systems are primarily found in older installations being upgraded.

**Can I monitor my home camera remotely in Singapore?**
Yes. All modern IP CCTV systems include remote monitoring via a mobile app. Footage is typically stored on a local network video recorder (NVR) at the property, with cloud backup available as an add-on. Ensure your home broadband upload speed is sufficient (minimum 5 Mbps recommended per camera for live viewing) and that your router's firewall and camera firmware are kept updated to prevent unauthorised access.

**What is an alarm monitoring contract?**
An alarm monitoring contract is a service agreement with a monitoring centre whereby the provider receives signals from your alarm system and dispatches a response when an alarm is triggered. Monthly fees in Singapore typically range from SGD 30–150 depending on the level of response and response time guarantee. Always read the contract term and termination notice period carefully before signing.

**How do I choose between an alarm system and CCTV?**
Alarm systems detect an intrusion and trigger a response in real time; CCTV records what happened before, during, and after an event. For comprehensive security, both are recommended. If budget requires a choice, alarm monitoring provides stronger real-time deterrence and response capability, while CCTV provides evidence value. Many Singapore providers offer bundled packages combining both at a reduced total cost.

**Are smart home security systems better than traditional systems?**
Smart home security systems — integrating IP cameras, smart locks, motion sensors, and alarm panels into a single mobile app — offer greater convenience and remote control than traditional standalone systems. However, their effectiveness depends on hardware quality and the reliability of your home internet connection. For critical security requirements, a professionally installed and monitored system from a provider like SECOM or Ademco will deliver more reliable performance than a self-installed smart home kit.
